Geologic history

Colorado Plateau

Zion is located on the western edge of the Colorado Plateau. The Colorado Plateaus spans across northern Arizona and New Mexico and southern Utah and Colorado. The Plateau is composed of one single contiental crust which was uplifted due to compressional forces. This uplifting event was caused by Laramide orogeny which was a mountain building event that started at 80 million years ago and ended 30 million years ago. This event was caused by the oceanic plate being subducted under the North american contineal plate. The Plateau was squeezed upwards but remain flat during this time. Mountains eroded there sediments towards the plateau which compacted forming the layers sedimentary layers. The eventually erosion of these sedimentary rocks would be called the Grand Staircase. The oldest rocks of the Grand Staircase can be found at the Grand Canyon while the youngest rocks can be found at Bryce Canyon. The Sediment Layers

The story of Zion started around 275 million years ago where it is situated as a basin near sea level, the Deposit of nearby materials cause the basin to sink making it a coastal plain during the Permian through the Jurassic leaving behind carbonate despot and mud, clay and sand on which later turned into Limestone, mudstone, shale and sandstone. These layers rose up from the Laramide Orogeny along with the rest of the colorado plateau. The Landscape of Zion was created by the erosion caused by this Orogeny upheaval then the Virgin River, a tributary of the Colorado River came in and later steepening the canyon.

Dinosaurs Fossils

The one of the most notable fossils of Zion are the Megapnosurus and Dilophosuas. Both are two legged and predatory dinosaurs with small forearms proportionate to their bodies. The Meganphons is a 70 lbs and 10 feet long and the Dilophosaurus is 20 feet long and weigh up to 1000 pounds. The fossil records of these Dinosaurs are preserved as trace fossils of their footprints in Zion. These Footprints can be found in the Kayenta and Moenave Rock layers, but please note These sites are fragile and difficult to access.

Old Fossils

we start to see some of the earliest forms of life on earth's record. This dates back 240 million years ago during the early Triassic period. The Fossils on this layer includes fossilized plants,footprints of the earliest lizards and Brittle Stars. During this Period Zion was a coastline which is the reason we see both land and water fossils. Then we go down to the Kalib layer which only includes life from the early Permian] such as brozayans, brachiopods, crinoids and trilobites. During this time the Zion Land mass was completely submerged in water.

Hydrologly

When there is a dessert there's a lot of opportunity for water to shape the landscape. As the Colorado River carved out the Grand Canyon so have the Virgin River with Zion. The Virgin River is a tributary of the colorado. It begins north at The Cascade falls, It travels down to lake mead where it join with the Colorado. The Virgin River's path starts at 9,000 feet above sea sea level it drops 7,800 feet as it travels down 160 miles a steep decline for a river. This Steepness allows the river to cut through Zion layers of sedimentary rocks with relative ease. You can Visit the north fork of the virgin river by hiking on the "the Narrows Path" The layers of Sand also makes it easy for water to travel down to the canyon which results in making senceine water falls in the late spring season
